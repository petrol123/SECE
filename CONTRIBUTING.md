# Contributing to SECE

Thank you for your interest in contributing to SECE (Synthetic Emotional Cognition Engine).  
This project is authored and stewarded by **Paul Totoritis**, and is currently in an early
public phase focused on documentation, architecture, and collaborative exploration.

SECE is an ongoing research and engineering effort aimed at developing emotionally
responsive, qualia-like feedback mechanisms for synthetic cognition. Contributions are
welcome from researchers, engineers, neuroscientists, and collaborators who share an
interest in ethical, transparent, and human-aligned AI systems.

SECE is currently in a **documentation-first** phase.

- Documentation
- Architecture discussions
- Research notes
- Conceptual modeling
- Issue reporting
- Proposals for modules or interfaces

## üìù How to Contribute

### 1. **Open an Issue**
If you have:
- A question  
- A proposal  
- A correction  
- A research insight  
- A suggestion for future modules  

Please open an issue.  
Use clear titles and provide context so discussions remain productive.

### 2. **Join Discussions**
SECE values thoughtful, interdisciplinary dialogue.  
Feel free to participate in open issues or start new threads.

### 3. **Submit Pull Requests (PRs)**
PRs are welcome for:
- Documentation improvements  
- Clarifications  
- Structural enhancements  
- Non-code contributions  
- Drafts of future modules (if appropriate)

If you plan a substantial contribution, please open an issue first to align direction.

---

## üìê Contribution Principles

All contributions should follow these principles:

### **Clarity**
Write with precision, transparency, and respect for interdisciplinary readers.

### **Integrity**
SECE prioritizes ethical development, emotional safety, and human alignment.

### **Modularity**
Proposed ideas or components should be structured, reusable, and well-scoped.

### **Respect**
Collaborators come from diverse backgrounds.  
Maintain professionalism and constructive dialogue.
